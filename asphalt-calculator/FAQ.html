Frequently Asked Questions - Advanced Bituminous Calculator

What is the Advanced Bituminous Calculator?
The Advanced Bituminous Calculator is a comprehensive web-based tool designed to assist engineers, contractors, and planners in estimating costs and quantities for bituminous road construction projects. It aligns with the Department of Roads (DoR) Road and Bridge standards, ensuring calculations reflect industry norms. The calculator handles:
  • Material Quantity: Calculates required materials and their quantities based on compacted volume and density.
  • Material Costs: Calculates total material costs by entering the per unit material cost at site as a whole or in disaggregated form like per unit material cost at source, transportation cost, driver wages, and fuel costs, etc.
  • Per Unit Cost at Site: Calculates per unit (e.g., kg, m³) costs at site by providing per unit cost at source, tipper rent per day, driver cost per day, fuel cost, distance (km), and mileage.
  • Select Best Tipper-truck: Calculates the best and cost-friendly tipper-truck (6-wheelers or 10-wheelers or any other) for material transportation by comparing the per unit cost at site from both vehicles.
  • Select Best Material Sources: If two sources are at different distances and different costs at sources, it calculates and recommends the best cost-friendly sources.
  • Production Costs: Calculates the total cost of work including material costs, transportation costs, vehicles and equipment costs, skilled and unskilled worker costs, and all from purchasing material to producing, laying, and finalizing bituminous work.
It provides detailed tables and comparisons to optimize project planning and budgeting.

How do I calculate transportation costs per m³?
Follow these steps to calculate transportation costs:
  1. Click "Calculate Material Cost Per m³" to expand the section
  2. Enter values in the appropriate fields:
    • Material Cost per m³ at Source: Direct input from supplier quotes
    • Transportation Parameters:
      • Tipper specifications (capacity, rental rate)
      • Driver costs (daily wage)
      • Trip details (distance, expected trips per day)
      • Fuel parameters (price, vehicle mileage)
  3. Click "Calculate" to view detailed results

Calculation Formulas:

// Tipper Rent per m³ Tipper Rent per m³ = Tipper Rent per Day / (Total Trips × Tipper Capacity) // Driver Cost per m³ Driver Cost per m³ = Driver Cost per Day / (Total Trips × Tipper Capacity) // Fuel Cost per m³ Fuel Cost per m³ = (Round Trip Length × Diesel Price) / (Mileage × Tipper Capacity)

Example Calculation:

Input Values: - One Round Trip Length = 20 km - Diesel Price = 150 Rs/L - Mileage = 2 km/L - Tipper Capacity = 7.5 m³ Calculation: Fuel Cost per m³ = (20 × 150) / (2 × 7.5) = 200.00 Rs/m³
