Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How accurate is the Land Area Converter?

The converter is designed to be as accurate as possible based on standard land measurement units used in Nepal.

Are there official area conversion rates in Nepal?

The 7th Amendment of the Jagga (Nap Jach) Ain, 2019 (Land Survey and Measurement Act, 1963) states that 10,000 square meters is equivalent to 1 hectare. The old measurements in Ropani and Bigha will be converted according to this new standard. The previous land conversion method (you can read more about it here) defined 1 Bigha as 270 ft x 270 ft, which equals 72,900 square feet. Similarly, 1 Ropani was defined as 74 ft x 74 ft, which equals 5,476 square feet. In the Ropani, Aana, Paisa, Daam system, 1 Ropani is equal to 16 Aana, 1 Aana equals 4 Paisa, and 1 Paisa equals 4 Daam. Likewise, in the Bigha, Kattha, Dhur system, 1 Bigha equals 20 Kattha, 1 Kattha equals 20 Dhur, and 1 Dhur equals 4 Kanawaa. To know about the calculation of the area of land, you can refer to the official document.

What land units can I convert?

You can convert between Ropani, Aana, Paisa, Daam, Bigha, Kattha, Dhur, Square Meters, Acres, and more.

Can I use fractional values for conversions?

Yes, the tool supports fractional values for accurate calculations.
