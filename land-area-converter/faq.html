Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - Nepali Land Area Converter

General Questions

What units are used in the Nepali Land Area Calculator?

The calculator supports two systems: Ropani (with Aana, Paisa, Daam) and Bigha (with Kaththa, Dhur)and all modern units like square feet, square meter, hector, acre, square yard, etc.. It allows addition or subtraction within each traditional system or mixed calculations between the two.

How accurate is the Land Area Converter?

The converter is designed to be highly accurate, using standard Nepali land measurement units and precise conversion ratios as defined in the Land Survey and Measurement Act, 1963.

Are there official area conversion rates in Nepal?

The 7th Amendment of the Jagga (Nap Jach) Ain, 2019 (Land Survey and Measurement Act, 1963) defines 10,000 square meters as 1 hectare. Traditional measurements like 1 Bigha (72,900 sq ft) and 1 Ropani (5,476 sq ft) are converted accordingly. In the Ropani system, 1 Ropani = 16 Aana, 1 Aana = 4 Paisa, 1 Paisa = 4 Daam. In the Bigha system, 1 Bigha = 20 Kaththa, 1 Kaththa = 20 Dhur. More details are available in the official document.

Can I use fractional values for conversions?

Yes, the calculator supports fractional values for precise calculations of land measurements.

Mixed Calculations and Usage

How does the calculator handle mixed Ropani and Bigha calculations?

The calculator converts all inputs to Daam (Ropani system: 1 Ropani = 256 Daam; Bigha system: 1 Dhur ≈ 8.5280896 Daam), performs additions or subtractions, and converts the total back to both Ropani-Aana-Paisa-Daam and Bigha-Kaththa-Dhur formats.

Can the calculator handle negative results?

No, negative land areas are invalid. The calculator displays an error if the result is negative, ensuring valid measurements.

How do I use the calculator to add or subtract land areas?

Select the Ropani, Bigha, or Mixed section. Enter values, choose 'Add' or 'Subtract,' add more input sets if needed, and click 'Calculate Total' to see results in both systems.
