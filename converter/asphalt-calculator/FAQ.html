FAQ - Advanced Bituminous Calculator

1. What is the Advanced Bituminous Calculator?
The Advanced Bituminous Calculator is a web-based tool designed to assist engineers, contractors, and planners in estimating costs and quantities for bituminous road construction projects. It aligns with the Department of Roads (DoR) Road and Bridge standards, ensuring calculations reflect industry norms. The calculator handles:
  • Material Costs: Based on volume, density, and rates.
  • Transportation Costs: Per m³ costs for tipper rent, driver, and fuel from two sources (Crusher 1 and Crusher 2).
  • Production Costs: Equipment usage costs (e.g., pavers, rollers).
It provides detailed tables and comparisons to optimize project planning.
2. Why is the Advanced Bituminous Calculator necessary?
This calculator is crucial for:
  • Cost Accuracy: Prevents under- or over-estimation of project budgets.
  • Compliance: Adheres to DoR standards for bituminous road construction.
  • Decision-Making: Compares costs from two crushers to choose the most economical source.
  • Resource Planning: Estimates material quantities and equipment needs.
Without such a tool, manual calculations are time-consuming and prone to errors, risking project delays or cost overruns.
3. What can i do with this calculator?
First, user can estimate the required quantities of materials for given compacted volume. If user needs, then, he can provide costs of materials in disaggregated form (to calculate this there is the calculate material cost per m3 button) or can put direct total per unit cost in one of the corresponding user filed. This brings the total costs of materials. Click on the Total Production cost button and put the cost/rent of the equipments and other costs per day, and it provides the total production costs including materials costs for your project.
4. How does the calculator work?
The calculator operates in three main sections:
  1. Estimate Materials Quantity: Users input the required compacted volume (m³) of material such as BM, or DBM and can estimate the required quantity of bitumen (in kg), aggregates and sands (in cubic meters).
  2. Materials Costs: Users enter the per unit cost of materials in sources, transportation costs, driver costs, and fuel parameters (trip length, mileage, diesel price). To make this simple, there is additional material costs calculator.
  3. Production Costs: Equipment rates and usage hours are input to estimate total production costs.
5. What are the key features of the calculator?
  • Material Cost Calculation: Adjusts for compaction and displays grand totals.
  • Transportation Cost Breakdown: Compares Crusher 1 and Crusher 2 costs per m³ in a table format.
  • Production Cost Estimation: Toggleable section for equipment costs.
  • User-Friendly Interface: Expandable sections with clear tables and a "Calculate" button for on-demand updates.
  • Cost Comparison: Shows which crusher is more expensive per m³.
  • DoR Recommendations: Works details, gradation tables, and other instructions are added from the DoR, STANDARD SPECIFICATIONS FOR ROAD AND BRIDGE WORKS 2073 (With amendment 2075).
6. How do I calculate transportation costs per m³?
Click "Calculate Material Cost Per m³" to expand the section. Enter values in the table:
  • Material Cost per m³ in Crusher: Direct input, copied to results.
  • Tipper Rent per m³: Tipper Rent per Day / (Total Tips * Tipper Capacity).
  • Driver Cost per m³: Driver Cost per Day / (Total Tips * Tipper Capacity).
  • Fuel Cost per m³: (One Round Trip Length * Diesel Price) / (Mileage * Tipper Capacity).
Click "Calculate" to see results. Example:
One Round Trip Length = 20 km, Diesel Price = 150 Rs/L, Mileage = 2 km/L, Capacity = 7.5 m³ Fuel Cost per m³ = (20 * 150) / (2 * 7.5) = 200.00 Rs/m³
7. How is the calculator implemented?
It’s built with:
  • HTML: Structures input fields and result tables.
  • CSS: Styles tables and buttons (e.g., teal headers, pink highlights).
  • JavaScript: Handles calculations and DOM updates.

8. What standards does it follow?
It’s based on Government of Nepal, Department of Roads,STANDARD SPECIFICATIONS FOR Road and Bridge, which typically include norms for material qualities, proportions, and instructions for the work. The calculator assumes these standards for bituminous mixtures, ensuring realistic cost estimates.
9. Can I customize the calculator?
Yes, you can put your own values like particular stone aggregate percentages, bulk density, bitumen content, etc. In Production Cost Chart, you can add extra items such as equipments and tools by clicking 'add new item'.
